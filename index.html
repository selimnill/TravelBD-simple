The origin of the word "travel" is most likely lost to history. The term "travel"
may originate from the Old French word travail, which means 'work'.[2] According to
the Merriam-Webster dictionary, the first known use of the word travel was in
the 14th century. It also states that the word comes from Middle English travailen, travelen
(which means to torment, labor, strive, journey) and earlier from Old French
travailler (which means to work strenuously, toil).
